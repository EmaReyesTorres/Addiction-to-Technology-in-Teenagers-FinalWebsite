Technology is a tool that, when used responsibly, can bring significant benefits in areas such as information transfer and acquisition, communication, work, learning and leisure. It also serves as a tool to improve the access of specific groups to specific cultural and social values.

New technologies and the risks of their use

However, the misuse and abuse of new technologies causes the opposite effect to that desired, involving the following risks:

○ Going from information to overstimulation and inhibition where the person becomes capable of processing so much information

○ Communication change by social isolation

○ Going from knowledge of realities to creating a virtual world of fiction and confusion

○ Have learning difficulties, especially in reading and writing

○ Change leisure for the abandonment of school, work and/or family obligations

In the case of adolescence, there is a higher risk of misusing and abusing them, as a consequence of having low self-esteem and being in a risky family situation that can range from lack of family communication to lack of trust from parents to adolescents. Likewise, it is a complicated stage due to the difficulty of accepting one's own body image, as well as belonging to a group and being constantly subjected to the pressure exerted by it.

Types of technological addictions

○ Video game addiction

○ Nomophobia or mobile phone addiction

○ Online gambling addiction

○ Social networks addiction

○ Online Shopping Addiction

○ Backlog depression

Treatment for addiction in technolgy.

Proceed to carry out a thorough evaluation to know every detail of the addiction you are working with. The questions, records or self-report are a key determinant to help the patient to know what her behavior is like. In this way, the patient can become aware of their situation and work on the areas that can help them overcome their addiction, be it to the mobile phone or the computer.

At this stage, the patient is helped to choose goals and objects to perform during the therapeutic process. It is not intended that the patient refrain from using the technologies in their entirety, since today it is practically impossible, but that they know how to control themselves.

One of the main objectives of the treatment of addiction to mobile phones, social networks or the Internet is to learn to make controlled use of them. For that reason, it's key to set limits and schedules for its use. In this way, it is avoided that the patient stops enjoying other activities in his daily life due to the prolonged use of technology.

The patient has the opportunity to gain quality of life and create happy moments. To achieve this, they are helped to develop a list of activities that allow them to create a healthier and more balanced lifestyle. Other methods include:

• Limit use to encourage self-regulation (1 to 2 hours daily)

• Foster relationships and communication through other channels

• Promote other types of activities (reading, painting, cinema...)

• Play team sports
